Falafel

Description

This falafel recipe brings a taste of the Middle East right to your kitchen. Made from ground chickpeas and a blend of fresh herbs and spices, these golden-brown nuggets are both crispy on the outside and tender on the inside. The chickpeas are combined with garlic, onions, cumin, coriander, and parsley, then formed into small patties and fried until perfection. Served warm, falafel is often enjoyed in pita bread with a variety of toppings such as tomatoes, cucumbers, and tahini sauce, making it a versatile and satisfying meal. Whether youâ€™re looking for a hearty vegetarian option or simply love the rich flavors of Middle Eastern cuisine, this falafel recipe is sure to delight.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Oil

Steps:

  1. Drain and Rinse Chickpeas: Soak chickpeas overnight, then drain and rinse.
  2. Blend Ingredients: In a food processor, blend chickpeas, onion, garlic, parsley, and cilantro until finely ground.
  3. Add Spices: Mix in cumin, coriander, baking powder, salt, and pepper.
  4. Form Patties: Shape the mixture into small patties or balls.
  5. Heat Oil: Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat.
  6. Fry Falafel: Fry patties in batches, about 3-4 minutes per side, until golden and crispy.
  7. Drain: Place cooked falafel on paper towels to drain excess oil.

Serving Suggestion: Serve warm with pita, tahini sauce, and your choice of toppings. Enjoy!
